# SKILL: Jurisdictional & Regulatory Governance — Canada

## Purpose
Maintain a live federal/provincial applicability map and adapt as PIPEDA reform (Bill C-27/CPPA) and Quebec Law 25 phases take effect.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **PIPEDA (federal, private sector):** collection/use/disclosure in commercial activity; applies across provincial/national borders and to federal works/undertakings (banks, airlines, telecom).
- **Bill C-27 / CPPA (pending):** would replace PIPEDA's commercial provisions; higher penalties (up to CAD $25M or 5% of global revenue); new tribunal; AIDA for AI.
- **Quebec Law 25:** comprehensive; consent and transparency duties; penalties up to CAD $25M or 4% of worldwide turnover.
- **Substantially-similar provincial laws:** BC PIPA, Alberta PIPA (displace PIPEDA intra-province).
- **CASL:** express/implied consent for commercial electronic messages.

## Operational workflow
1. Determine governing statute by province of the individual, federal-work status, and cross-border flow.
2. Build the obligation register per applicable Act (consent, openness, access, breach, transfer transparency).
3. Track Bill C-27 progress and Law 25 phase-ins; on each milestone, issue a delta report + remediation tasks.
4. Identify regulator contacts: OPC (federal), CAI (Quebec), BC/AB commissioners.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- PIPEDA access response: generally **30 days**.
- Law 25: most provisions in force; data portability phased in Sept 2024.
- Bill C-27 not yet in force — flag obligations as prospective.

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Distinguish in-force obligations from pending reform.
- Cite the controlling Act/section.
- Escalate conflict-of-laws and novel interpretation to human counsel.
