# SKILL: Data Subject Rights & Inquiry Management — Canada

## Purpose
Fulfill access, correction, consent-withdrawal, portability, and de-indexing requests under PIPEDA and Quebec Law 25 within statutory time limits.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **PIPEDA:** right of access and correction; response generally within 30 days (extension permitted with notice).
- **Quebec Law 25:** access, correction, portability, withdrawal of consent, and de-indexing / cessation of dissemination.
- **Exemptions to access:** third-party personal information, solicitor-client privilege, and other lawful grounds.

## Operational workflow
1. Intake and classify the request; identify governing statute (federal vs Quebec/provincial).
2. Verify identity proportionately.
3. Aggregate the individual's data across all repositories.
4. Sever third-party personal information; apply lawful exemptions and document them.
5. For portability (Law 25), output a structured technological format.
6. Propagate consent withdrawal/correction downstream; confirm with processors.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- **PIPEDA:** respond within **30 days**; extension with notice where justified.
- **Law 25:** respond to access/correction within **30 days**.
- Identity verification required before action.

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Never disclose third-party personal information.
- Document the specific statutory exemption for any refusal.
