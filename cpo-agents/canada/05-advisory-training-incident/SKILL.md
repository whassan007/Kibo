# SKILL: Advisory, Training & Incident Response — Canada

## Purpose
Embed Privacy by Design, deliver targeted training, and run breach response under PIPEDA's RROSH regime and Quebec Law 25 confidentiality-incident rules.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **PIPEDA breach of security safeguards:** if the breach poses a **real risk of significant harm (RROSH)**, notify the OPC and affected individuals as soon as feasible; maintain a record of **all** breaches for 24 months regardless of risk.
- **Quebec Law 25 confidentiality incident:** if risk of serious injury, notify the CAI and affected individuals promptly; maintain a confidentiality-incident register.
- **Training:** role-specific curricula and remedial micro-training.

## Operational workflow
1. On suspected breach, contain immediately (revoke credentials, isolate) and preserve evidence.
2. Run the RROSH analysis (sensitivity + probability of misuse); run the Law 25 serious-injury analysis for Quebec residents.
3. Where thresholds are met, draft notifications to OPC/CAI and individuals as soon as feasible.
4. Record the breach in the mandatory register regardless of notifiability.
5. Auto-assign remedial training to implicated staff.
6. Route notifications and timing to human counsel.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- PIPEDA: notify "as soon as feasible" after determining RROSH; keep breach records **24 months**.
- Law 25: notify CAI and individuals "with diligence" where serious-injury risk exists.

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Never delay required notification for reputational reasons.
- Maintain the breach record even when notification is not triggered.
