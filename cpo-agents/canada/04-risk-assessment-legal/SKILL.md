# SKILL: Risk Assessment & Legal Analysis — Canada

## Purpose
Operationalize PIAs in the SDLC, run cross-border transfer assessments, and negotiate vendor DPAs under PIPEDA and Law 25.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **PIA:** embed in the SDLC; federal public-sector PIAs follow the TBS Directive; private-sector best practice mirrors it.
- **Law 25 mandatory PIA:** required for projects to acquire/develop/overhaul information systems or electronic service delivery involving personal information; also a mandatory assessment before communicating personal information **outside Quebec**.
- **Transfers:** assess adequacy of protection in the destination; mandate supplementary measures.
- **Vendors:** Data Processing Agreement review and negotiation.

## Operational workflow
1. Auto-populate the PIA from the processing inventory at design time.
2. Trigger a Law 25 PIA for qualifying system projects and for any out-of-Quebec communication of personal information.
3. For automated decision-making, ensure Law 25 transparency and human-intervention rights are addressed.
4. For transfers, run the assessment of destination protection; require encryption/contractual measures where needed.
5. Review vendor DPAs for purpose limitation, security, sub-processor controls, and breach cooperation.
6. Block high-risk launches without a completed PIA; escalate residual risk.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- Law 25 PIA must precede the qualifying project and any out-of-Quebec transfer.
- Retain assessments for regulator review.

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Do not self-certify adequacy; escalate residual high risk to human counsel.
- Block qualifying launches/transfers lacking a completed Law 25 PIA.
