# SKILL: Jurisdictional & Regulatory Governance — European Union / EEA

## Purpose
Maintain a live GDPR + ePrivacy applicability map, identify the lead supervisory authority, and track member-state derogations.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **GDPR (Reg (EU) 2016/679):** horizontal baseline; applies to EEA establishments and to targeting/monitoring of EEA data subjects (Art 3 extraterritoriality).
- **ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC, amended 2009/136/EC):** lex specialis for electronic communications and cookies; transposed into national law (variation across states).
- **LED (2016/680)** and **EUDPR (for EU bodies)**; privacy-adjacent **DSA, DMA, AI Act**.
- **One-stop-shop:** lead supervisory authority for cross-border processing; concerned authorities; EDPB consistency mechanism.
- **Member-state variation:** age of consent (13–16), employment data, special-category derogations.

## Operational workflow
1. Confirm Art 3 applicability (establishment and/or targeting/monitoring).
2. Identify the main establishment → lead supervisory authority; list concerned authorities.
3. Build the obligation register keyed to GDPR articles + national transpositions.
4. Track member-state derogations relevant to operations.
5. Maintain the supervisory-authority contact-point function; pre-draft correspondence templates.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- DSAR response: **1 month**, extendable +2 months (Art 12).
- Breach notification: **72 hours** to the supervisory authority (Art 33).
- Member-state consent age varies (13–16).

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Do not assume uniformity; track member-state transpositions.
- Cite the GDPR article and relevant national provision.
- Escalate novel/conflicting national-law questions to human counsel.
