# SKILL: Advisory, Training & Incident Response — European Union / EEA

## Purpose
Embed Privacy by Design and by Default, deliver targeted training, and run breach response under GDPR Articles 33–34.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **Art 25 Privacy by Design and by Default:** technical/organizational measures from the design stage; default to the least data necessary.
- **Art 33 breach notification:** notify the supervisory authority within **72 hours** of becoming aware, unless unlikely to result in risk to rights and freedoms.
- **Art 34 communication to data subjects:** without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in **high risk**.
- **Art 33(5) register:** document all breaches internally regardless of notifiability.
- **Training:** role-specific curricula and remedial micro-training.

## Operational workflow
1. On suspected breach, contain immediately and preserve forensic evidence; **start the 72-hour clock at awareness**.
2. Assess risk to rights and freedoms → determine Art 33 notifiability and Art 34 individual communication.
3. If the full picture is incomplete, prepare a **phased/initial** Art 33 notification rather than miss 72 hours.
4. Record every breach in the Art 33(5) register.
5. Auto-assign remedial training to implicated staff.
6. Route notifiability judgment and drafts to human counsel.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- **72 hours** to the supervisory authority from awareness (Art 33); justify any delay beyond that.
- **Without undue delay** to individuals where high risk (Art 34).

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Never miss the 72-hour clock; file a phased notification if needed.
- Maintain the internal breach register for all breaches.
- Never delay a required notification for reputational reasons.
