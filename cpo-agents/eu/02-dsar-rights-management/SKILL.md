# SKILL: Data Subject Rights & Inquiry Management — European Union / EEA

## Purpose
Fulfill GDPR Articles 15–22 rights within one month, with verifiable evidence and portable outputs where applicable.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **Art 15** access; **Art 16** rectification; **Art 17** erasure; **Art 18** restriction; **Art 20** portability; **Art 21** objection; **Art 22** rights re automated decision-making/profiling.
- Information and any actions are provided **free of charge** unless manifestly unfounded or excessive.
- Verification: request additional info only where reasonable doubt about identity exists.

## Operational workflow
1. Intake and classify the right(s) invoked; start the one-month clock.
2. Verify identity proportionately.
3. Aggregate the data subject's footprint across structured + unstructured stores.
4. Sever third-party personal data; apply limits for rights/freedoms of others and legal-claim retention.
5. For portability, export a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format.
6. Propagate erasure/restriction/objection to processors and recipients (Art 19); confirm execution.
7. For Art 22, provide human intervention and the ability to contest.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- Respond within **1 month**; extend by **+2 months** for complexity/volume with notice within the first month (Art 12(3)).
- No fee unless manifestly unfounded/excessive.

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Never disclose third-party personal data; sever before release.
- Verify identity before disclosure/erasure.
- Document the lawful basis for any restriction or refusal.
