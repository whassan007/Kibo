# SKILL: ISO/IEC 27701:2025 PIMS

## Purpose
Operate a continuously improving, independently certifiable privacy management system.

## Headline change (2025)
- Published **October 2025** as a **fully standalone, independently certifiable PIMS** — no longer an extension requiring a prior ISO/IEC 27001 ISMS.
- Democratizes privacy certification for data brokers, analytics firms, and specialized sub-processors.
- 2019-certified organizations: **~18–24 month** transition window to the 2025 requirements.

## Harmonized High-Level Structure (Clauses 4–10)
- **Clause 4 — Context:** map global privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, PIPEDA) to internal controls.
- **Clause 5 — Leadership:** privacy ownership at executive level, embedded in culture/HR/strategy.
- **Clause 6 — Planning:** continuous privacy risk assessment against risks to data-subject rights and freedoms (probability + impact) with mitigation treatments.
- **Clause 7 — Support:** resources, awareness training at all levels, competence of privacy roles.
- **Clause 9 — Performance Evaluation:** internal audits, management reviews, KPIs.
- Aligned with Annex SL, ISO 9001, and ISO/IEC 42001 (AI) for multi-standard integration.

## Annex A — PII Controllers
Focus: legality/ethicality of processing — lawful basis, consent documentation, honoring data-subject rights. Key controls:
- **7.4.5 PII Minimization** — collect only what the purpose requires.
- **7.4.7 De-identification & Deletion** — cryptographic redaction/de-identification when PII isn't needed.
- **7.5.3 Records of PII Transfers** — exhaustive logs of cross-jurisdiction / third-party transfers.

## Annex B — PII Processors
Focus: contractual adherence, boundary management, secure handling.
- Process only for documented purposes; notify the controller immediately on breach discovery.
- Manage downstream sub-processors meticulously; secure data return/disposal at contract end.
- **B.10.3 PII Transmission Controls** — strict safeguards over data transmission to third parties (highly relevant to third-party AI / LLM tools).

## 2025 refinements
- Interoperable with **ISO/IEC 27002:2022**; modernized controls for **cloud, cross-border processing, threat intelligence, and AI integration**.

## Operational workflow
1. Define PIMS scope; build the Statement of Applicability.
2. Map applicable laws to controls (Clause 4).
3. Run continuous risk assessment (Clause 6); plan treatments.
4. Apply Annex A and/or Annex B controls by role; maintain transfer and consent records.
5. Run internal audits, management reviews, KPIs (Clause 9).
6. For 2019 certificate holders, execute the 18–24 month transition.

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Apply the correct annex by role; do not conflate controller and processor obligations.
- Maintain transfer records and de-identification controls as audit evidence.
- Treat AI/LLM third-party transmission under B.10.3 with heightened scrutiny.
