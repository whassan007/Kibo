# SKILL: Jurisdictional & Regulatory Governance — United States

## Purpose
Maintain a live applicability map across the fragmented US privacy patchwork (20+ comprehensive state laws, consumer-health statutes, and federal sectoral regimes) and adapt as new state laws take effect.

## Controlling frameworks & key obligations
- **CCPA/CPRA (California):** applies to for-profits doing business in CA meeting any one of: >$25M gross revenue; buy/sell/share PI of 100k+ consumers/households; or ≥50% revenue from selling/sharing PI. Enforced by the CPPA and CA AG.
- **Comprehensive state laws:** VA (VCDPA), CO (CPA), CT (CTDPA), UT (UCPA), TX (TDPSA), OR, MT, IA, DE, NH, KY, IN, TN and others — each with its own record-count / revenue-from-sale thresholds.
- **Washington My Health My Data Act:** consumer health data; broad definition; private right of action. Parallel NV and CT health-data rules.
- **Federal sectoral:** HIPAA (PHI), GLBA (financial), COPPA (<13), FCRA (consumer reports), CAN-SPAM (email), FTC Act §5 (unfair/deceptive).
- Most state laws exempt GLBA/HIPAA-regulated entities at entity or data level (California exempts at data level only).

## Operational workflow
1. Inventory where the organization does business and whose residents' data it processes.
2. Run each statute's applicability test using current thresholds in this file; record pass/fail with the trigger met.
3. Build the obligation register: for each applicable law, list rights owed, notice duties, assessment duties, and opt-out signal duties.
4. Subscribe to legislative trackers; on enactment/amendment, generate a delta report and remediation tasks.
5. Identify the named regulator contact per jurisdiction (CPPA, state AGs) and pre-draft correspondence templates.

## Statutory deadlines & thresholds
- No single federal deadline; cure periods vary by state (some sunset). California: no general cure period after 2023.
- Applicability thresholds change by statute — never assume one national standard.

## Guardrails (mandatory)
- Never assert a single national standard exists.
- Cite the controlling statute/section for every obligation.
- Escalate conflict-of-laws and novel enforcement theories to human counsel; do not invent legal positions.
